# 15 - Incubation and Quality Control

Once inoculated, your media requires time, specific temperatures, and monitoring to fully colonize. 

## The Incubation Environment
* **Temperature:** Maintain a steady **70°F to 75°F**. (Temperatures above 80°F drastically increase the chance of bacterial growth).
* **Lighting:** Keep all incubating media in a dark space, such as a closed closet or cabinet. 
* **Airflow:** Ambient air exchange is fine, but keep jars out of direct drafts.

## 1. Agar Incubation
* Stack plates upside down (lid on bottom) to prevent condensation from dripping onto the agar surface.
* Monitor daily. Healthy mycelium is pure white and ropey or fluffy.
* **Contamination Check:** If you see green, pink, black, or slimy wet spots, you must immediately isolate the plate and perform an Agar-to-Agar transfer (See Phase 4).

## 2. Liquid Culture Incubation
* Run the LC jar on the magnetic stir plate for **10 minutes every day**. This breaks the mycelium up and forces it to multiply aggressively rather than forming a single solid puck.
* It should be fully colonized and cloudy with suspended white mycelium in 10 to 14 days.

## 3. Grain Spawn Incubation (The Break & Shake)
1.  Place inoculated grain jars on a shelf. Do not disturb them for the first 7-10 days. 
2.  **The 30% Mark:** Once the jar is roughly 30% covered in white mycelium, it is time for the "Break and Shake."
3.  Vigorously shake the jar, or strike it against a bicycle tire or your hand, until the white clump is completely broken apart and the colonized kernels are evenly distributed throughout the jar.
4.  This accelerates growth. The jar should fully colonize and turn 100% white within the next 5 to 7 days.
5.  **Contamination Check:** Look for "wet rot" (grains pressed against the glass that look slimy and melted) or green/grey powders. If a jar is contaminated, do not open it indoors. Discard the contents outside.
