# 18 - Grain Spawn Incubation

Grain spawn requires the longest incubation time and relies on passive gas exchange through the filter patch to survive.

## The Environment
* **Temperature:** Maintain a steady **70°F to 75°F**. 
* **Storage:** Keep jars upright on a shelf in a dark room. Ensure there is ambient air exchange in the room, but do not place a fan blowing directly on the jars, as this can push contaminants through the filters.

## The Growth Timeline & The "Break and Shake"
1.  Do not disturb the jars for the first **7 to 10 days** to allow the mycelium to establish a strong foothold.
2.  **The 30% Mark:** Monitor the jars. Once the popcorn is roughly 30% covered in white mycelium, it is time for the "Break and Shake."
3.  Vigorously shake the jar, or strike it against a bicycle tire or your palm, until the white clump is completely broken apart and the colonized kernels are evenly distributed throughout the jar.
4.  This spreads the growth points. The jar should fully colonize and turn 100% white within the next **5 to 7 days**.

## Quality Control
* **Spotting Contamination:** Look for "wet rot" (grains pressed against the glass that look slimy, dark, and melted) or green/grey powdery spots (Trichoderma). 
* **Action:** If a jar is contaminated, **do not open it indoors under any circumstances**. Take the jar outside, dump the contents into the trash, and bleach the jar thoroughly.
