# 08 - Still Air Box (SAB) Workflow

Moving air carries contamination. The SAB keeps air currents still. You must move slowly and carefully the entire time your arms are inside.

## Preparation
1.  Place all items (jars, agar, scalpel, rack) inside the SAB.
2.  Spray the inside walls, floor, roof, and air with 70% alcohol. Spray jars and agar cups as well.
3.  Wipe the alcohol from the roof (so it doesn't drip) and from the viewing area.
4.  **Do not wipe the walls and floor.** Keep them wet to catch floating particles.
5.  Wipe the tops of the jar lids to remove excess alcohol so it doesn't soak into the filter patches.
6.  Let the SAB sit for **30 minutes** before using so contaminants settle to the floor.

## Movement Rules
* **Stay Elevated:** Keep your arms and hands elevated and away from the floor so you don't swish up contamination.
* **No Hovering:** Always keep your hands from hovering or passing over open work. Contam falls off hands and tools.
* **Speed:** Move slowly and fluently. Minimize the time any container is open and exposed.
* **Tool Handling:** Use a block on your raised tray to sit tools down without them touching a surface. Only hold the upper handle of the tool. The closer you grab toward the tip, the higher the contam risk.
* **Blade Contact:** Only touch the agar with the very tip of the sterile blade. Do not touch your tools to anything other than the target object.
