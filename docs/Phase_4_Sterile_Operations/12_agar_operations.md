> [!NOTE]
> Agar is a 2D growing plane used to germinate spores, verify liquid culture purity, and isolate strong mycelial growth away from contamination. All work must be performed inside the Still Air Box (SAB).

### 💧 Inoculating Agar (Spores or Liquid Culture)
1. Inside the SAB, flame sterilize your syringe needle until glowing red, then let it cool for 3 seconds.
2. Open the agar cup/plate just wide enough to slip the needle in.
3. Drop exactly **1 or 2 drops** of the liquid onto the center of the agar. (Injecting too much liquid will cause pooling and invite bacterial blooms).
4. Close the plate immediately. Wait for growth (usually 3-7 days).

### 🔪 Agar to Agar Transfers (Cleaning Genetics)
If your agar plate shows healthy white mycelium but also exhibits spots of mold or bacterial slime, you must transfer the clean growth to a new plate to save the genetics.

1. Flame sterilize your scalpel blade until glowing red. Cool it by touching it to a blank, clean spot on the receiving agar plate.
2. Open the colonized plate. Carefully cut out a tiny wedge (the size of a grain of rice) from the **leading edge** of the healthiest white mycelium, staying far away from any contamination.
3. Spear the wedge with the tip of the scalpel.
4. Quickly open the fresh agar plate, drop the wedge directly into the center, and close it.
5. Repeat this process on new plates until you have a plate that grows 100% clean, aggressive mycelium with zero contamination.
