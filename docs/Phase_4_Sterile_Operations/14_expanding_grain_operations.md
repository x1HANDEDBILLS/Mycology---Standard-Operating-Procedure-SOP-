> [!NOTE]
> This is the process of introducing clean mycelium into sterilized popcorn jars to create spawn, and multiplying existing spawn to scale up your operation exponentially.

### ✔️ Pre-Inoculation Checklist
* Receiving grain jars have rested for 1 week and show no signs of internal contamination.
* SAB has been prepped, sprayed, and rested for 30 minutes.
* Tools have been sterilized via torch (glowing red) and cooled.

### 🔪 Method A: Agar to Grain Transfer
1. Inside the SAB, loosen the lid of the receiving grain jar slightly to prepare for opening.
2. Open the fully colonized, clean agar cup/plate. Using your scalpel, quickly cut the agar into small wedges (tic-tac-toe pattern).
3. Spear 2 or 3 agar wedges with the tip of the scalpel.
4. Lift the lid of the grain jar just enough to slip the scalpel inside. Drop the wedges onto the grain.
5. Close the grain jar immediately. Repeat for additional jars.
6. Tighten all jar lids and shake gently to distribute the agar wedges among the kernels.

### 💉 Method B: Liquid Culture to Grain (Syringe Injection)
> [!TIP]
> This is the fastest and least risky method since the jar never has to be opened to the ambient air.

1. Inside the SAB, thoroughly wipe the injection port on the grain jar lid with a fresh alcohol wipe.
2. Flame sterilize the needle of your LC syringe until glowing red. Let it cool in the air for 3 seconds.
3. Pierce the injection port and inject **1.5cc to 2cc** of liquid culture directly into the grain.
4. Withdraw the needle. The self-healing port will seal itself automatically.
5. Shake the jar well to coat as many kernels as possible with the liquid culture.

### 🌽 Method C: Grain-to-Grain (G2G) Transfer
> [!IMPORTANT]
> Use one fully colonized "Master Jar" to inoculate up to 10 brand-new, sterile grain jars. This creates explosive, exponential growth because you are transferring hundreds of live inoculation points.

1. Before entering the SAB, vigorously break and shake your fully colonized Master Jar so all the kernels are loose.
2. Inside the SAB, slightly loosen the lids of the Master Jar and your sterile receiving jars.
3. Take the lid off the first receiving jar and the Master Jar.
4. Carefully pour a small amount (roughly 1 to 2 tablespoons) of colonized grain from the Master Jar directly into the receiving jar. 
5. Quickly replace both lids. 
6. Repeat for remaining receiving jars. 
7. Once finished, tighten all receiving jar lids and shake them well to distribute the colonized kernels evenly among the sterile kernels.
