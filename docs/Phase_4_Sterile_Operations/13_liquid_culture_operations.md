> [!TIP]
> Liquid culture takes a small, verified-clean piece of mycelium from an agar plate and multiplies it infinitely in a suspended sugar water matrix. 

### 🔪 Inoculating the Liquid Culture
1. Perform this inside the SAB. **Ensure your agar plate is 100% fully colonized and perfectly clean.**
2. Using a flame-sterilized scalpel, cut a small wedge of the clean agar.
3. Swiftly open the lid of your sterilized LC jar, drop the agar wedge inside, and seal the lid immediately.
4. Place the jar on your magnetic stir plate for 5 minutes to break up the mycelium wedge. 

### 🔬 Extraction and Testing (Mandatory)
> [!WARNING]
> Never, under any circumstances, inject grain with Liquid Culture until you have tested it on agar to ensure it is 100% clean. The fluid hides contamination well.

1. Inside the SAB, vigorously wipe the injection port of the fully colonized LC jar with a fresh alcohol wipe.
2. Flame sterilize an 18-gauge needle attached to a sterile syringe.
3. Pierce the port, draw up 1cc of fluid, and place a few drops onto a clean agar plate.
4. Wait 3-5 days for incubation and verification (See Phase 5). If the agar plate grows healthy mycelium with zero bacteria or mold, the entire LC jar is verified safe to draw into syringes and use on your grain jars.
