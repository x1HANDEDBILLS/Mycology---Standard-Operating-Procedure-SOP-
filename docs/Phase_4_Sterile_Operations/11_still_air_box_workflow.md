  <h1>🥽 11 - Still Air Box Workflow</h1>
  <i>Phase 4: Sterile Operations</i>
</div>

---

> [!CAUTION]
> Moving air carries contamination. The SAB is designed to keep air currents dead still. You must move slowly and deliberately the entire time your arms are inside the chamber.

### 🧼 Preparation
1. Place all required items (jars, agar, scalpel, raised rack) inside the SAB.
2. Spray the inside walls, floor, roof, and ambient air heavily with 70% isopropyl alcohol. Spray jars and agar cups as well.
3. Wipe the alcohol from the roof (so it doesn't drip onto open agar) and from your viewing window.

> [!IMPORTANT]
> **Do not wipe the walls and floor.** Keep them soaking wet to catch and trap any floating particles.

5. Wipe the tops of the jar lids to remove excess alcohol so it doesn't soak into the filter patches.
6. Let the SAB sit completely untouched for **30 minutes** before using so all contaminants settle to the wet floor.

### 🦾 Movement Rules
* **Stay Elevated:** Keep your arms and hands elevated and away from the floor so you don't swish up settled contamination.
* **No Hovering:** Always keep your hands from hovering or passing over open work. Contam falls off hands and gloves.
* **Speed:** Move slowly and fluently. Minimize the exact seconds any container is open and exposed.
* **Tool Handling:** Use a block on your raised tray to sit tools down without them touching a wet surface. Only hold the upper handle of the tool. The closer you grab toward the tip, the higher the contam risk.
* **Blade Contact:** Only touch the agar with the very tip of the sterile blade. Do not touch your tools to anything other than the target object.
