  <h1>🍄 20 - Harvesting and Drying</h1>
  <i>Phase 7: Harvest & Processing</i>
</div>

---

> [!IMPORTANT]
> This is a standard baseline protocol. Edit these temperature and timing settings based on the airflow strength of your specific dehydrator model.

### 🔪 Harvesting
1. Mushrooms are at peak readiness to harvest when the veil underneath the cap stretches thin and begins to tear away from the stem.
2. Wash and sterilize your hands or wear gloves.
3. Grasp the mushroom as close to the substrate as possible. Gently twist and pull to release it from the coco coir without tearing up large chunks of the substrate matrix.
4. Use a soft brush or a scalpel to scrape away any remaining coco coir attached to the bottom of the stem. Do not wash the mushrooms with water.

### 🏜️ Drying
1. Place the cleaned mushrooms onto your dehydrator trays. Ensure they are not overlapping to allow for maximum airflow.
2. Set the dehydrator temperature between **125°F and 165°F**. (Lower temps take longer but are safer for preserving delicate active compounds; higher temps are faster and prevent rot).
3. Run the dehydrator continuously for 12 to 24 hours.

> [!TIP]
> **The Cracker Test:** The mushrooms are completely dry when the thickest part of the stem snaps immediately like a dry saltine cracker when bent. If it bends, flexes, or feels spongy at all, put it back in the dehydrator.

5. Store cracker-dry product in airtight glass mason jars with desiccant silica packets in a cool, dark place.
