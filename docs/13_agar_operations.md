# 13 - Agar Operations

Agar is a 2D growing plane used to germinate spores, verify liquid culture purity, and isolate strong mycelial growth away from contamination. All work must be performed inside the Still Air Box (SAB).

## Inoculating Agar (Spores or Liquid Culture)
1.  Flame sterilize your syringe needle until glowing red, then let it cool for 3 seconds.
2.  Open the agar cup/plate just wide enough to slip the needle in.
3.  Drop exactly **1 or 2 drops** of the liquid onto the center of the agar. (More liquid will pool and invite bacteria).
4.  Close the plate immediately. Wait for growth (usually 3-7 days).

## Agar to Agar Transfers (Cleaning Genetics)
If your agar plate shows healthy white mycelium but also spots of mold or bacterial slime, you must transfer the clean growth to a new plate.
1.  Flame sterilize your scalpel blade until glowing red. Cool it by touching it to a blank spot on the clean receiving agar plate.
2.  Open the colonized plate. Carefully cut out a tiny wedge (the size of a grain of rice) from the **leading edge** of the healthiest white mycelium, staying far away from any contamination.
3.  Spear the wedge with the scalpel.
4.  Quickly open the fresh agar plate, drop the wedge into the center, and close it.
5.  Repeat this process until you have a plate that grows 100% clean mycelium with zero contamination.
