# 05 - Prep Agar Media

*Note: This recipe is scaled for a 500ml media bottle, filling it to 400ml to make pouring easier.*

## Mixing the Media
1.  Add **400ml** of water (RODI preferred) into a Qt mason jar. Get the water hot (but not boiling) to help mix ingredients.
2.  Add a magnetic stir bar.
3.  Add **8g Light Malt Extract (LME)** and mix well.
4.  Add **8g Agar** and mix well.

## Dissolving (Microwave Method)
1.  Heat the jar in the microwave on high for about 3 minutes, hand stirring occasionally.
2.  Watch closely: Keep heating until it starts to expand and boil up. Stop the microwave immediately before it boils over.
3.  Repeat this heating and boiling up effect a few times until hot and dissolved.

## Bottling and Sterilizing
1.  Place the jar on a magnetic stirrer and mix thoroughly.
2.  Pour the mixture into the 500ml media bottle. *(Optional: Strain with cheesecloth for best clarity).*
3.  Add the stir bar into the media bottle. Stir on low to remove air bubbles. *(Optional: Add 1-3 drops of food coloring).*
4.  Fully tighten the lid on the media bottle (no foil needed).
5.  Place the bottle in the pressure cooker on a trivet. Add water up to 1 inch below the agar line.
6.  Vent for 10 minutes, apply weight, and pressure cook on low for **20 minutes @ 15 psi**.
7.  Let depressurize for 45 minutes.

## Cooling
1.  Remove and place on the magnetic stirrer on **low**. (Stirring too vigorously will create bubbles and ugly pours).
2.  Agar is ready to pour when it cools to **120°F** (or when you can comfortably hold the bottle bare-handed for 30 seconds).
