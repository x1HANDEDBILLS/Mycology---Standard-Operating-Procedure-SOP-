# 05 - PC Calibration and Water Test

Before risking your materials in a live run, perform a blank water test. This establishes a baseline calibration for your specific stove and helps you understand the thermal mass and equilibrium of the pressure cooker.

## The Objective
To learn the auditory cues of the pressure weight and find the exact stove dial setting that maintains a steady-state 15 PSI without boiling the pot dry.

## The Physics of Venting
A pressure cooker relies on pure steam. If you do not vent the ambient air out of the pot before building pressure, the trapped air will create "cold spots" inside the cooker, resulting in false pressure readings and unsterilized grain.

## Step 1: The Setup
1.  Add **8 cups of plain water** to the empty cooker.
2.  Lock the lid in place. Ensure the venting pipe is clear and the rubber gasket is seated.
3.  Turn your stove burner on **High**.

## Step 2: The Venting Phase
1.  Watch the visual safety lock. It will eventually pop upward, sealing the lid.
2.  Once a steady stream of visible steam is shooting out of the vent pipe, start a timer for **10 minutes**.
3.  Let it blow continuously. This aggressively purges all the cold air from the chamber.

## Step 3: Finding Equilibrium
1.  After 10 minutes, place the 15-PSI weight onto the vent pipe using an oven mitt.
2.  The cooker will now build pressure. Wait until the weight begins to aggressively dance and spit steam.
3.  Now, incrementally dial the heat down. **Wait 5 minutes between adjustments** to allow the thermal mass to catch up to the new heat setting.
4.  **Your Target:** The weight should settle into a gentle, rhythmic wobble (about 1 to 2 rocks per second). It should just barely be pushing air out. 
5.  *Warning:* If it wobbles violently, you are losing water. If it stops moving completely, you have dropped below 15 PSI.

## Step 4: Marking Your Baseline
1.  Once you find that perfect equilibrium dial position, mark it on your stove with a piece of tape. This is your permanent "15 PSI Hold" setting.
2.  Turn off the heat and let the cooker depressurize naturally until the safety lock drops.
