# 13 - Inoculation Procedures

*Note: This is a standard baseline protocol. Edit these steps to match your specific transfer strategy.*

## Pre-Inoculation Checklist
* Grain jars have rested for 1 week and show no signs of contam.
* SAB has been prepped, sprayed, and rested for 30 minutes.
* Tools have been sterilized via torch (glowing red) and cooled.

## Method A: Agar to Grain Transfer
1.  Inside the SAB, loosen the lid of the receiving grain jar slightly to prepare for opening.
2.  Open the agar cup/plate. Using your scalpel, quickly cut the agar into small wedges.
3.  Spear an agar wedge with the tip of the scalpel.
4.  Lift the lid of the grain jar just enough to slip the scalpel inside. Drop the wedge onto the grain.
5.  Close the grain jar immediately. Repeat for additional jars.
6.  Tighten all jar lids and shake gently to distribute the agar wedges.

## Method B: Syringe to Grain (Liquid Culture / Spores)
1.  Ensure injection ports on jar lids were properly sterilized with an alcohol wipe.
2.  Attach a sterile needle to your syringe. 
3.  Flame sterilize the needle until glowing red. Let it cool for 3 seconds.
4.  Pierce the injection port and inject 1-2cc of liquid into the grain.
5.  Withdraw the needle. The self-healing port will close.
6.  Shake the jar to distribute the liquid.
