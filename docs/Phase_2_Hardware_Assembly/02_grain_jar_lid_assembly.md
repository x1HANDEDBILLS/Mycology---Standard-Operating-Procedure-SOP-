  <h1>🫙 02 - Grain Jar Lid Assembly</h1>
  <i>Phase 2: Hardware Assembly</i>
</div>

---

> [!NOTE]
> **The Goal:** Grain spawn requires **high passive gas exchange** to colonize properly. The primary focus of a grain lid is maximum airflow **without** allowing airborne mold spores inside.

<br>

## **📐 Layout Options**

* **Option A: Agar-to-Grain (Filter Only)**
  If you exclusively use agar wedges to inoculate grain, you only need filter patches. Drill **2 holes** and apply **2 patches**. *(If using the high-flow Microppose brand filters, just **1 hole** is sufficient).*

* **Option B: Syringe-to-Grain (Filter + Port)**
  If you plan to inject liquid culture directly into the grain, drill **1 hole** for a filter patch, and **1 hole** for a self-healing injection port.

<br>

## **🛠️ Drilling Procedure**

1. Screw a plastic/metal lid onto an empty jar for structural stability.
2. Drill **1/4-inch holes** for the filter patches.
3. If adding an injection port, drill a **7/16-inch hole**.

> [!WARNING]
> Ensure the center of any hole is at least **3/4 of an inch away from the very edge** of the lid. If it is too close, the modifications will interfere with the sealing gasket and ruin the jar's seal.

<br>

## **🧩 Assembly**

1. Clean the holes of any plastic or metal burrs using an Exacto blade.
2. Wash all lids heavily with soap to remove drill debris and let them dry **completely**.
3. If using an injection port, force it into the **7/16-inch hole**. It will be a tight fit; pulling it through from the inside with pliers helps. Ensure it is fully seated and perfectly level.
4. Peel and stick the adhesive filter patches evenly over the **1/4-inch holes**.
