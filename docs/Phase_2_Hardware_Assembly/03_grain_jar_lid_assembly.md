  <h1>🫙 03 - Grain Jar Lid Assembly</h1>
  <i>Phase 2: Hardware Assembly</i>
</div>

---

> [!NOTE]
> Grain spawn requires high passive gas exchange to colonize properly. The primary focus of a grain lid is maximum airflow without allowing airborne mold spores inside.

### 📐 Layout Options
* **Agar-to-Grain (Filter Only):** If you exclusively use agar wedges to inoculate grain, you only need filter patches. Drill 2 holes and apply 2 patches. *(If using high-flow Microppose brand filters, 1 hole is sufficient).*
* **Syringe-to-Grain (Filter + Port):** If you plan to inject liquid culture directly into the grain, drill 1 hole for a filter patch and 1 hole for a self-healing injection port.

### 🛠️ Drilling Procedure
1. Screw a metal lid onto an empty jar for structural stability.
2. Drill **1/4-inch holes** for the filter patches.
3. If adding an injection port, drill a **7/16-inch hole**.

> [!WARNING]
> Ensure the center of any hole is at least **3/4 of an inch away from the very edge** of the lid so the modifications do not interfere with the sealing ring/gasket.

### 🧩 Assembly
1. Clean the holes of any plastic or metal burrs using an Exacto blade.
2. Wash all lids with soap to remove drill debris and let them dry completely.
3. If using an injection port, force it into the 7/16-inch hole. It will be a tight fit; pulling it through from the other side with pliers helps. Ensure it is fully seated and level.
4. Peel and stick the adhesive filter patches evenly over the 1/4-inch holes.
