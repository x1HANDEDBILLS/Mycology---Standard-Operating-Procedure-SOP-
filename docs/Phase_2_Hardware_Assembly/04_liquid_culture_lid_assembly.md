# 04 - Liquid Culture Lid Assembly

Liquid culture requires a completely sealed environment that allows for pressure equalization during sterilization, and sterile extraction of the fluid later on.

## Layout Requirements
Every Liquid Culture lid **must** have exactly one filter patch (for pressure equalization) and exactly one self-healing injection port (for syringe extraction).

## Drilling Procedure
1.  Screw a lid onto an empty jar for stability.
2.  Drill a **1/4-inch hole** for the filter patch.
3.  Drill a **7/16-inch hole** for the injection port.
4.  **Important:** Keep the holes evenly spaced and ensure the center of the holes are at least **3/4 of an inch away from the edge** of the lid.

## Assembly
1.  Clean the holes of any burrs using an Exacto blade.
2.  Wash the lid to remove all debris and let it dry completely.
3.  Push the self-healing injection port into the 7/16-inch hole. Make sure it forms a tight, perfect seal so no liquid or air can escape around the edges.
4.  Apply the adhesive filter patch over the 1/4-inch hole.
