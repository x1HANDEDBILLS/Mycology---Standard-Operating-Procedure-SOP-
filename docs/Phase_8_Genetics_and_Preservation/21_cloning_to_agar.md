  <i>Phase 8: Genetics & Preservation</i>

---

> [!NOTE]
> **The Goal:** Cloning allows you to replicate the exact genetics of your best, fastest-growing, or largest mushroom (clusters). By doing this, you close the loop and never need to purchase spores again.

<br>

## **🧼 Preparation**

1. Select the most aggressive/best mushroom from your harvest *before* it goes into the dehydrator.
2. Place the fresh mushroom, your scalpel, and fresh agar plates inside the Still Air Box (SAB).
3. Follow standard SAB sterilization protocols (spray down, wait **30 minutes**).

<br>

## **🔪 The Extraction Protocol**

> [!WARNING]
> The outside of the mushroom is covered in ambient bacteria from the open air. The inside of the stem is perfectly sterile. You must extract inner tissue **without** letting the outside touch the agar.

1. Inside the SAB, flame sterilize your scalpel until glowing red. Cool it in the air.
2. Grasp the base of the mushroom stem. Using your hands *(not the blade)*, physically **tear the mushroom in half** vertically down the stem. This exposes the sterile, untouched inner tissue.
3. Using the tip of your sterile scalpel, carefully carve out a **tiny piece of tissue** from the very center of the torn stem.
4. Quickly drop that inner tissue onto the center of a fresh agar plate and close the lid.
5. Incubate the plate at **72°F**. White mycelium will grow aggressively outward from the tissue, giving you an exact genetic clone of that specific mushroom.
