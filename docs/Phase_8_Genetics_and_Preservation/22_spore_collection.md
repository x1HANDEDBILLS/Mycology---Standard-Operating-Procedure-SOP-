> [!TIP]
> Spores are nature's ultimate long-term storage. Unlike live, watery mycelium, spores can survive extreme conditions and last for decades. Collecting spores ensures you always have a baseline genetic backup of your harvest.

<br>

## **📄 Method A: Spore Prints**
*Spore prints are ideal for mushrooms with flat, wide caps that drop heavy spore loads.*

1. Select a mature mushroom where the veil has completely broken and dropped.
2. Inside the Still Air Box (SAB), use a sterile scalpel to cut the cap off the stem as close to the gills as possible.
3. Place a piece of heavy-duty aluminum foil (wiped with alcohol and dried) flat on your SAB surface.
4. Place the mushroom cap, **gills facing down**, directly onto the foil.
5. Cover the cap with a clean glass or small tupperware container to prevent any micro air movement.
6. Wait **12 to 24 hours**.
7. Remove the glass and carefully lift the cap. You will see a dark, dense print on the foil.
8. Fold the foil over to protect the print, seal the edges, place it inside a ziplock bag, and store it in a cool, dark, dry place. 

<br>

## **🧬 Method B: Spore Swabs**
*Swabs are necessary for specific mutant strains or unique mushroom geometries that do not naturally drop spores well onto flat surfaces.*

1. Inside the SAB, open a pack of sterile medical cotton swabs (Q-tips).
2. Hold the mature mushroom cap securely.
3. Take the sterile swab and firmly rub it between the gills of the mushroom until the cotton tip is **visibly dark and coated with spores**.
4. Place the swab immediately back into its sterile plastic tube or wrapper, seal it, and store it in a cool, dry place.
