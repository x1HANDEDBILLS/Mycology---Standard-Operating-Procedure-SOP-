# 12 - Liquid Culture Operations

## Inoculating the Liquid Culture
1.  Perform this inside the SAB. Ensure your agar plate is fully colonized and clean.
2.  Using a flame-sterilized scalpel, cut a small wedge of clean agar.
3.  Swiftly open the lid of your sterilized LC jar, drop the agar wedge inside, and seal the lid immediately.
4.  Place the jar on your magnetic stir plate for 5 minutes to break up the mycelium. 

## Incubation
1.  Store the jar in a dark room at 70°F - 75°F.
2.  Run the jar on the magnetic stir plate for **10 minutes every day**. This breaks the mycelium up and forces it to multiply aggressively rather than forming a single solid puck.
3.  It should be fully colonized and cloudy with mycelium in about 10 to 14 days.

## Extraction and Testing (Mandatory)
*Never inject grain with LC until you have tested it on agar to ensure it is 100% clean.*
1.  Inside the SAB, wipe the injection port of the LC jar with an alcohol wipe.
2.  Flame sterilize an 18-gauge needle attached to a sterile syringe.
3.  Pierce the port, draw up 1cc of fluid, and place a few drops onto a clean agar plate.
4.  Wait 3-5 days. If the agar plate grows healthy mycelium with zero bacteria/mold, the entire LC jar is safe to draw into syringes and use on your grain.
