> [!NOTE]
> This recipe yields exactly 6 perfectly hydrated Quart jars of yellow popcorn. 

### 💧 Hydrating the Popcorn
1. Fill the 16 qt pressure cooker with 20 cups of water. Heat on high and bring to a roaring boil.
2. Add **1/2 cup of corn syrup** (use a splash of hot PC water in the measuring cup to help dissolve it).
3. Add **4 lbs of yellow popcorn**.
4. Seal the PC lid. Vent for 10 minutes at 5-8 psi, place weight.
5. Pressure cook on low for **15 minutes @ 15 psi**.
6. Turn heat off and let depressurize naturally for 45 minutes.

### 💨 Drying
1. Pour the hot corn into a large strainer and let sit for 1 to 2 hours. 
2. **NO RINSING.** Let the radiant heat and steam naturally evaporate the water off the kernels.
3. Every 30 minutes, toss the popcorn from the strainer to a bowl and back to evenly mix them.
4. The outside of the grain must be bone-dry to the touch. You should have absolutely no water on your hand when you mix it up. (Expect 90 minutes and 3 tosses).

### 🌡️ Sterilizing the Jars
1. Jar up the popcorn. Close your assembled grain lids fully and tight (no foil needed).
2. Place jars in the PC raised off the bottom with a trivet. Add 1/2 inch bolts under the trivet to give it extra clearance off the heating base.
3. Add **16 cups of water** to the PC.
4. Vent for 10 minutes. Pressure cook for **2 hours @ 15 psi**.
5. Let it cool overnight with the PC lid securely on. 

### ⚖️ Post-Cook Conditioning
1. Once completely cool, shake jars every 6 to 12 hours for 24-48 hours to redistribute the remaining internal moisture. 

> [!WARNING]
> **Safety Rule:** DO NOT HOT SHAKE. The glass can shatter, and the kernels will turn to mush. Let the jars cool fully before shaking them. The popcorn will shake just fine 12 hours after you PC them.

3. Let the jars sit for a week before use to verify proper sterilization (ensure nothing unwanted is growing inside).
