> [!TIP]
> **The Goal:** Liquid Culture (LC) is a massive efficiency multiplier. It allows you to turn a single small piece of clean mycelium into dozens of syringes of rapid-colonizing fluid. 

<br>

## **🧪 The Recipe (4% Sugar Solution)**

1. Add **400ml of hot water** (RODI preferred) into a Quart mason jar.
2. Add **1 Tablespoon of Light Corn Syrup**. *(Alternatively, you can use exactly **1 gram** of LME or Peptone mix).*
3. Add a magnetic PTFE stir bar directly into the jar.
4. Stir vigorously until the thick syrup is completely dissolved and integrated into the water matrix.

<br>

## **🌡️ Bottling and Sterilizing**

1. Attach your assembled LC jar lid *(Must have a self-healing injection port and a **0.22um** filter patch).*
2. Ensure the lid is closed securely. You absolutely do not want dirty PC water boiling into your pristine nutrient solution.
3. Place the jar in the pressure cooker on a trivet.
4. Vent for **10 minutes**, apply the weight, and pressure cook on low for **20 minutes @ 15 PSI**.

> [!WARNING]
> **Do not** pressure cook LC for longer than **20 minutes**. If overcooked, the suspended sugars will caramelize, turn dark brown, and completely ruin the nutritional profile.

5. Let the cooker depressurize naturally and allow the jar to cool completely to **room temperature** before attempting to inoculate it.
