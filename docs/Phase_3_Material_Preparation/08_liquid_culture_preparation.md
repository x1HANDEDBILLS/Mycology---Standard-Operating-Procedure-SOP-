> [!TIP]
> Liquid Culture (LC) is an efficiency multiplier. It allows you to turn a single small piece of clean mycelium into dozens of syringes of rapid-colonizing fluid. 

### 🧪 The Recipe (4% Sugar Solution)
1. Add **400ml of hot water** (RODI preferred) into a Quart mason jar.
2. Add **1 Tablespoon of Light Corn Syrup**. *(Alternatively, you can use exactly 1 gram of LME).*
3. Add a magnetic stir bar directly into the jar.
4. Stir vigorously until the thick syrup is completely dissolved and integrated into the water. 

### 🌡️ Bottling and Sterilizing
1. Attach your assembled LC jar lid (Must have an injection port and a filter patch).
2. Ensure the lid is closed securely. You absolutely do not want dirty PC water boiling into your pristine nutrient solution.
3. Place the jar in the pressure cooker on a trivet.
4. Vent for 10 minutes, apply the weight, and pressure cook on low for **20 minutes @ 15 psi**.

> [!WARNING]
> Do not pressure cook LC for longer than 20 minutes. If overcooked, the suspended sugars will caramelize, turn dark brown, and ruin the nutritional profile.

5. Let the cooker depressurize naturally and allow the jar to cool completely to room temperature before attempting to inoculate it.
