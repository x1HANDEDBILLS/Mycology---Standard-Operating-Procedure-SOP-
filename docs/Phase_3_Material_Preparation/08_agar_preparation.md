  <h1>🧫 08 - Agar Preparation</h1>
  <i>Phase 3: Material Preparation</i>
</div>

---

> [!NOTE]
> This recipe is scaled specifically for a 500ml media bottle, filling it only to 400ml to make pouring clean and easy without spilling.

### 🧪 Mixing the Media
1. Add **400ml** of water (RODI preferred) into a Qt mason jar. Get the water hot (but not boiling) to help mix ingredients.
2. Add a magnetic stir bar into the jar.
3. Add **8g Light Malt Extract (LME)** and mix well.
4. Add **8g Agar powder** and mix well.

### ☢️ Dissolving (Microwave Method)
1. Heat the jar in the microwave on high for about 3 minutes, hand stirring occasionally.
2. **Watch closely:** Keep heating until it starts to expand and rapidly boil up the sides. Stop the microwave immediately before it boils over.
3. Repeat this heating and "boiling up" effect a few times until the powder is fully dissolved and the liquid is translucent.

### 🌡️ Bottling and Sterilizing
1. Place the jar on a magnetic stirrer and mix thoroughly.
2. Pour the mixture into the 500ml media bottle. *(Optional: Strain through cheesecloth for absolute maximum clarity).*
3. Add the stir bar into the media bottle. Stir on low to remove air bubbles. *(Optional: Add 1-3 drops of food coloring).*
4. Fully tighten the lid on the media bottle (no foil needed).
5. Place the bottle in the pressure cooker on a trivet. Add water to the PC up to 1 inch below the agar line.
6. Vent for 10 minutes, apply weight, and pressure cook on low for **20 minutes @ 15 psi**.
7. Let depressurize naturally for 45 minutes.

### ❄️ Cooling
1. Remove the bottle and place it on the magnetic stirrer on **low**. (Stirring too vigorously will whip bubbles into the gel, creating ugly pours).
2. Agar is ready to pour into cups when it cools to exactly **120°F** (or when you can comfortably hold the glass bottle bare-handed for 30 seconds).
