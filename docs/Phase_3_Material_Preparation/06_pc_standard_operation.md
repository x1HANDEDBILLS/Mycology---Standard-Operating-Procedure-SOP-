  <h1>⏱️ 06 - PC Standard Operation</h1>
  <i>Phase 3: Material Preparation</i>
</div>

---

> [!WARNING]
> **Critical Rule:** Running out of water during a 2-hour cook will permanently warp and destroy the pressure cooker. **Never bypass** the 10-minute venting protocol, and **never cook on High heat** once pressure is reached.

<br>

## **1️⃣ Setup**

1. Add the **required amount of water** for your specific recipe (usually between **8 to 16 cups**, depending on the run length).
2. Place your jars/bottles inside on a **raised trivet** *(add 1/2 inch bolts under the trivet if you need extra clearance from the heating base)*.
3. Ensure the rubber gasket is installed and lock the lid. Turn heat to **High**.

<br>

## **2️⃣ Venting Protocol**

1. Wait for the safety valve to pop upwards, locking the lid securely.
2. Once popped, let steam blow out the vent pipe continuously for **10 minutes** to purge all internal air.
3. Place the **15-PSI weight** on the nipple.

<br>

## **3️⃣ The Cook**

1. When the weight starts to wobble, immediately turn the heat down to your pre-calibrated **"15 PSI Hold"** setting.
2. Start your recipe's timer *only* after the weight has established a steady, rhythmic rock.
3. Monitor the cooker periodically to ensure the wobble remains constant.

<br>

## **4️⃣ Depressurization**

1. When the timer finishes, turn the heat completely **Off**.

> [!CAUTION]
> **Never force-cool the cooker.** Do not run it under cold water, and do not remove the weight early. The sudden drop in pressure will cause the liquid inside your jars to boil over violently. Let it sit until the safety valve drops naturally (usually **45 to 60 minutes**).

<br>
