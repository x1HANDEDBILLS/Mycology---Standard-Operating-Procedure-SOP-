  <h1>🌡️ 05 - PC Calibration & Water Test</h1>
  <i>Phase 3: Material Preparation</i>
</div>

---

> [!IMPORTANT]
> **The Goal:** Before risking your expensive materials in a live run, perform a blank water test. This establishes a permanent baseline calibration for your specific stove, and helps you understand the thermal mass of the cooker.

<br>

## **🔬 The Physics of Venting**

A pressure cooker relies on **100% pure steam** to reach **250°F**. If you do not vent the ambient air out of the pot *before* building pressure, the trapped air will create "cold spots" inside the cooker, resulting in false pressure readings and completely unsterilized grain.

<br>

## **1️⃣ The Setup**

1. Add **8 cups of plain water** to the empty cooker.
2. Lock the lid in place. Ensure the venting pipe is perfectly clear and the rubber gasket is seated flat.
3. Turn your stove burner onto **High**.

<br>

## **2️⃣ The Venting Phase**

1. Watch the visual safety lock. It will eventually pop upward, sealing the lid tight.
2. Once a steady, powerful stream of visible steam is shooting out of the vent pipe, start a timer for **10 full minutes**.
3. Let it blow continuously. This aggressively purges all the cold ambient air from the chamber.

<br>

## **3️⃣ Finding Equilibrium**

1. After **10 minutes**, place the **15-PSI weight** onto the vent pipe using an oven mitt.
2. The cooker will now build pressure. Wait until the weight begins to aggressively dance and spit steam.
3. Now, incrementally dial the heat down. **Wait 5 full minutes between adjustments** to allow the thick metal thermal mass to catch up to the new heat setting.

> [!TIP]
> **Your Target:** The weight should settle into a gentle, rhythmic wobble (about **1 to 2 rocks per second**). It should just barely be pushing air out. 
> * **Violent wobble:** You are losing water too fast and will boil dry. 
> * **No wobble:** You have dropped below 15 PSI and lost sterilization temps.

<br>

## **4️⃣ Marking Your Baseline**

1. Once you find that perfect equilibrium dial position, mark it on your stove with a piece of tape or a sharpie. This is your permanent **"15 PSI Hold"** setting.
2. Turn off the heat and let the cooker depressurize naturally until the safety lock drops.

<br>
