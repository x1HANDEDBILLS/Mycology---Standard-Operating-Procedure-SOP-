  <i>Phase 5: Incubation & Quality Control</i>

---

> [!NOTE]
> Agar requires a stable, dark environment to allow spores or mycelium to germinate and run completely across the surface of the plate.

### 🌡️ The Environment
* **Temperature:** Maintain a steady **70°F to 75°F**.
* **Storage:** Keep plates in a dark space (closet, cabinet, or opaque box).

> [!WARNING]
> **Stacking Protocol:** Always stack your agar plates or cups **upside down** (with the lid on the bottom). This prevents condensation from forming on the lid and dripping down onto the agar surface, which immediately invites bacterial growth.

### 🔬 Quality Control & Monitoring
* Check plates daily. Healthy mycelium will appear as pure white, ropey (rhizomorphic), or fluffy (tomentose) growth.
* **Spotting Contamination:** If you see green, pink, or black spots, or slimy, shiny wet spots (bacteria), the plate is contaminated.
* **Action:** You must immediately isolate the plate and perform an Agar-to-Agar transfer (See Phase 4) to rescue the healthy leading edge of the mycelium away from the contam.
